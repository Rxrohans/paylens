PAYPAL INDIA — FEE STRUCTURE
Source: https://www.paypal.com/in/webapps/mpp/paypal-fees
=======================================================================

DOMESTIC TRANSACTIONS (sender and receiver both in India):
- Sending money to friends/family (personal): No fee if funded by PayPal balance or bank account
- Receiving money for goods/services (commercial): 2.5% of transaction amount

INTERNATIONAL TRANSACTIONS (sender and receiver in different countries):
- Definition: A transaction where sender and receiver are registered in different markets/countries
- Base transaction fee: 4.4% of the amount received
- Fixed fee: Varies by currency (e.g., 0.30 USD for USD transactions)
- Additional currency conversion fee: 3% to 4% above the base exchange rate

CURRENCY CONVERSION:
- PayPal adds a fee ABOVE the base exchange rate (mid-market rate)
- For most currencies: 3% above base exchange rate
- For some currencies: 4% above base exchange rate
- Example: If base rate is 1 USD = 83 INR, PayPal rate may be 1 USD = 80.5 INR (approx 3% spread)
- This conversion fee is how PayPal makes money on currency exchange

RECEIVING PAYMENTS FROM ABROAD (e.g., from Outlier AI, US clients):
- You pay: 4.4% transaction fee + fixed fee + currency conversion spread
- Example: Receive USD 100 → PayPal deducts ~4.4% = USD 4.40 → then converts remaining at 3% below market rate
- Total effective cost: approximately 7-8% of the received amount

PAYPAL WITHDRAWAL TO INDIAN BANK:
- Withdrawing INR to Indian bank: Free (if above minimum threshold)
- Minimum withdrawal: INR 100
- Processing time: 3-5 business days

PAYPAL DISPUTE/CHARGEBACK FEE:
- If a buyer files a claim and wins: Seller pays USD 20 (or local equivalent) chargeback fee
- This is in addition to refunding the transaction amount

STRIPE INDIA — FEE STRUCTURE
Source: https://stripe.com/in/pricing
=======================================================================

STANDARD PRICING (Pay-as-you-go):
- Cards issued in India: 2% per transaction
- Cards issued outside India (international cards): 3% per transaction
- No setup fees, no monthly fees, no hidden fees
- No closure fees

CURRENCY CONVERSION:
- If currency conversion is required: Additional 2% fee on top of transaction fee
- Example: Indian customer paying with USD card → 3% (international) + 2% (conversion) = 5% total

SPECIFIC PAYMENT METHODS:
- UPI payments in India: 2% per transaction (same as domestic cards)
- Net Banking: 2% per transaction
- Cards (Visa/Mastercard/RuPay issued in India): 2% per transaction
- American Express: Higher rates apply (contact Stripe)

FAILED PAYMENTS:
- Stripe does NOT charge for failed payment attempts
- You only pay fees on successful transactions

DISPUTES AND CHARGEBACKS:
- Dispute fee: USD 15 per dispute (approximately INR 1,250)
- Fee is refunded if you win the dispute
- Dispute fee applies regardless of transaction currency

PAYOUTS (receiving your Stripe balance):
- Standard payout to Indian bank: Free
- Payout schedule: T+2 (2 business days after transaction)
- Instant payouts: Available for eligible accounts, small fee applies

STRIPE RADAR (Fraud detection):
- Included free with standard pricing
- Advanced Radar: USD 0.02 per transaction additional fee

RAZORPAY INDIA — FEE STRUCTURE
Source: https://razorpay.com/pricing/
=======================================================================

STANDARD PRICING:
- All domestic transactions: 2% per transaction
- International cards: 3% per transaction
- No setup fee for standard account

PAYMENT METHOD BREAKDOWN:
- UPI (BHIM, Google Pay, PhonePe via Razorpay): 0% fee (as per NPCI guidelines, UPI is free for peer-to-peer; merchant rates apply for business)
- Net Banking: 2% per transaction
- Credit/Debit Cards (Domestic): 2% per transaction
- Credit/Debit Cards (International): 3% per transaction
- Wallets (Paytm, MobiKwik, etc.): 2% per transaction
- EMI transactions: 2% + EMI processing fee

GST ON FEES:
- All Razorpay fees are subject to 18% GST
- Example: 2% fee on INR 1000 = INR 20 → GST on INR 20 = INR 3.60 → Total deducted = INR 23.60

INSTANT SETTLEMENTS:
- Standard settlement: T+2 (2 business days)
- Instant settlement available: 0.25% to 1.5% additional fee depending on plan
- Razorpay X (current account): Free instant settlement

REFUNDS:
- Full refunds: No fee charged (Razorpay refunds the transaction fee too)
- Partial refunds: Transaction fee not refunded

INTERNATIONAL PAYMENTS ACCEPTANCE:
- Accept international cards: 3% fee
- Requires additional KYC and activation
- Currency conversion handled by bank at prevailing rates

=======================================================================
NPCI / UPI — FEE STRUCTURE
Source: NPCI guidelines
=======================================================================

PEER-TO-PEER (P2P) UPI TRANSFERS:
- Sending money to a person via UPI (Google Pay, PhonePe, BHIM): FREE
- No transaction fee for amounts up to INR 1 lakh per transaction
- No daily limit fee

MERCHANT PAYMENTS (P2M) via UPI:
- Transactions below INR 2,000: FREE (0% MDR as per RBI mandate)
- Transactions above INR 2,000: Up to 0.3% MDR (Merchant Discount Rate) may apply
- Prepaid Payment Instruments (PPI wallets) via UPI: 1.1% MDR above INR 2,000

FAILED TRANSACTION REVERSAL:
- Failed UPI transactions: Amount reversed within 2-3 business days
- No charge for failed transactions
- Bank may charge for excess failed transaction attempts in some cases

=======================================================================
COMMON FEE TERMS EXPLAINED
=======================================================================

MDR (Merchant Discount Rate):
- The fee a merchant pays to accept digital payments
- Charged as a percentage of each transaction
- Split between payment gateway, acquiring bank, and card network

Transaction Exchange Rate:
- The rate at which PayPal/Stripe converts your money
- Always slightly worse than the mid-market (interbank) rate
- The difference between mid-market rate and the offered rate IS the currency conversion fee

Chargeback:
- When a customer disputes a transaction with their bank
- Bank reverses the charge and the merchant is debited
- Merchant also pays a chargeback fee to the payment gateway

Settlement:
- When the payment gateway transfers collected money to your bank account
- Standard settlement: T+2 (2 business days after transaction date)
- Instant settlement: Same day or next day, usually for an extra fee

PPI (Prepaid Payment Instrument):
- Prepaid wallets like Paytm, MobiKwik
- Loaded in advance and used for payments
- Subject to different fee structures than bank-linked UPI

GST on Payment Fees:
- All payment processing fees in India attract 18% GST
- This is charged ON TOP of the base fee
- Example: 2% base fee on INR 10,000 = INR 200 → GST = INR 36 → Total fee = INR 236
