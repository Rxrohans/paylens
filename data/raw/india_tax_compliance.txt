GST ON PAYMENT PROCESSING FEES
=======================================================================

GST RATE ON FINANCIAL SERVICES: 18%
This applies to ALL payment processing fees in India.

HOW IT AFFECTS YOU:
- Razorpay charges 2% base fee → 18% GST on that fee → Total = 2.36%
- Stripe charges 2% base fee → 18% GST on that fee → Total = 2.36%
- Example on INR 10,000 transaction:
  Base fee = INR 200
  GST on fee = INR 36
  Total deducted = INR 236 (not INR 200)

GST ON PAYPAL:
- PayPal is an international service
- PayPal does NOT charge GST separately — it's embedded in the conversion spread
- If you're a GST-registered business, you cannot claim input credit on PayPal fees
- Stripe and Razorpay DO provide GST invoices → you CAN claim input credit

INCOME TAX ON FREELANCE / ONLINE INCOME
=======================================================================

IS FREELANCE INCOME TAXABLE?
Yes. 100%. Under "Profits and Gains from Business or Profession" (PGBP).

TAX SLABS (New Regime FY 2024-25):
- Up to INR 3 lakh: 0%
- INR 3-7 lakh: 5%
- INR 7-10 lakh: 10%
- INR 10-12 lakh: 15%
- INR 12-15 lakh: 20%
- Above INR 15 lakh: 30%
- Rebate: No tax if total income below INR 7 lakh (Section 87A)

DEDUCTIONS AVAILABLE FOR FREELANCERS:
- Internet connection charges: 100% deductible
- Computer/laptop/equipment: Depreciation deductible
- Software subscriptions: 100% deductible
- Home office (proportion of rent/electricity): Partially deductible
- Professional development (courses, books): Deductible
- Payment processing fees (Stripe/Razorpay fees): Deductible as business expense

PRESUMPTIVE TAXATION (Section 44ADA) — GREAT FOR FREELANCERS:
- If professional income < INR 75 lakh per year
- You declare 50% of gross receipts as profit automatically
- No need to maintain detailed books of accounts
- Pay tax on 50% of income, rest assumed to be expenses
- Example: Earned INR 10 lakh → Taxable income = INR 5 lakh
- File ITR-4 for this scheme

=======================================================================
TDS (TAX DEDUCTED AT SOURCE) FOR ONLINE INCOME
=======================================================================

TDS FROM INDIAN PLATFORMS:
- If a company (e.g., Indian client) pays you > INR 30,000 in a year for professional services
- They must deduct TDS @ 10% (Section 194J)
- They give you Form 16A
- You claim this TDS credit while filing ITR

TDS FROM FOREIGN CLIENTS (Outlier, US companies):
- Foreign companies are NOT required to deduct Indian TDS
- You receive full payment, NO TDS deducted
- You must calculate and pay advance tax yourself quarterly
- Advance tax due dates: 15 Jun (15%), 15 Sep (45%), 15 Dec (75%), 15 Mar (100%)
- If you miss advance tax and pay later: Interest @ 1% per month (Section 234B/C)

INWARD REMITTANCE TAX TREATMENT:
- Money received from abroad for services = Export of Services
- If GST registered: 0% GST on export of services (zero-rated)
- Income tax: Still applicable (income is taxable regardless of source)
- FIRC (Foreign Inward Remittance Certificate): Your proof of foreign income
  → Keep all FIRCs — needed for tax assessment

=======================================================================
GST REGISTRATION FOR FREELANCERS
=======================================================================

WHEN IS GST REGISTRATION MANDATORY?
- If total annual income exceeds INR 20 lakh (services)
- If providing services to overseas clients: Registration required if income > INR 20 lakh
- Special category states (NE states, Uttarakhand, etc.): INR 10 lakh threshold

EXPORT OF SERVICES — GST TREATMENT:
- Services provided to overseas clients = Export of Services
- Export of Services = Zero-Rated Supply under GST
- Zero-rated means GST rate = 0%, but you can claim refund of GST paid on inputs
- Example: You pay 18% GST on software subscriptions → you can claim refund

GSTIN BENEFITS FOR FREELANCERS:
- Can claim input tax credit on business expenses (Razorpay fees, software, etc.)
- Required for invoicing to Indian companies above threshold
- Appears professional to clients
- Easier compliance vs informal cash dealings

HOW TO FILE:
- GSTR-1: Monthly/quarterly sales return (export invoices)
- GSTR-3B: Monthly summary return (even if zero sales)
- LUT (Letter of Undertaking): File annually to export services without paying GST
  (Without LUT, you pay 18% GST and later claim refund — more hassle)

=======================================================================
PRACTICAL TAX TIPS FOR OUTLIER/ONLINE PLATFORM WORKERS
=======================================================================

1. OPEN A SEPARATE BANK ACCOUNT for all freelance income
   - Makes tracking easy for tax purposes
   - Separates personal and business money

2. TRACK ALL PAYMENT FEES as deductible expenses
   - PayPal: Take screenshots of fee deductions monthly
   - Stripe/Razorpay: Download monthly statements
   - These are legitimate business expenses

3. DOCUMENT YOUR FIRC/PAYMENT PROOF
   - Every international payment should generate an FIRC from your bank
   - Ask your bank for FIRC or download from net banking
   - Required for LRS compliance and potential tax scrutiny

4. QUARTERLY ADVANCE TAX (if income > INR 1 lakh/year from freelancing)
   - Estimate annual income
   - Pay 15%/45%/75%/100% by the due dates
   - Avoids interest penalties

5. MAINTAIN SIMPLE BOOKS:
   - Date of payment
   - Amount in foreign currency
   - Amount converted to INR (exchange rate)
   - Platform fees deducted
   - Net amount received
   - Excel sheet is sufficient for presumptive taxation scheme

6. CONSIDER HIRING A CA ONCE YOU CROSS INR 5 LAKH ANNUAL INCOME
   - Cost: INR 3,000 to 10,000 per year for basic filing
   - Worth it for compliance peace of mind
   - Can find one at ClearTax, IndiaFilings, or local CA

COMMON MISTAKES FREELANCERS MAKE
=======================================================================

MISTAKE 1: Not reporting foreign income
- All foreign income is taxable in India
- Income Tax dept can track inward remittances through banking data
- Penalty for under-reporting: 50% to 200% of tax due

MISTAKE 2: Confusing TCS and TDS
- TCS (Tax Collected at Source): On outward remittances (sending money abroad)
- TDS (Tax Deducted at Source): Indian clients deduct from payments to you
- Foreign clients do NOT deduct Indian TDS

MISTAKE 3: Not saving FIRCs
- FIRC is your proof of export of services
- Required if GST department asks for proof
- Keep digital copies of all FIRCs for 7 years

MISTAKE 4: Thinking PayPal fees are not taxable expenses
- PayPal fees are legitimate business expenses
- Document: Amount received before fees, fees deducted, net received
- Deductible under PGBP (Profits and Gains from Business/Profession)

MISTAKE 5: Not filing advance tax on time
- Even if you're in 0% slab, file advance tax return as nil if applicable
- Non-filing can attract notices even for zero tax
