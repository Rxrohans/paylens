HOW A PAYMENT ACTUALLY WORKS — END TO END
=======================================================================

When you pay someone online, this is what happens in milliseconds:

STEP 1 — AUTHORIZATION:
- Customer enters card/UPI details
- Payment gateway (Razorpay/Stripe) encrypts and sends to acquiring bank
- Acquiring bank contacts card network (Visa/Mastercard/RuPay)
- Card network contacts issuing bank (your bank)
- Issuing bank checks: sufficient balance? fraud signals? account standing?
- Issuing bank responds: Approved or Declined
- This entire process takes 2-3 seconds

STEP 2 — CAPTURE:
- After authorization, the merchant "captures" the amount
- Money is not yet moved — it is only "held" or "reserved"
- Some gateways auto-capture, others require manual capture

STEP 3 — CLEARING:
- At end of day, all transactions are batched and sent for clearing
- Card networks (Visa, Mastercard) process and reconcile transactions
- This is when the actual transfer instructions are created

STEP 4 — SETTLEMENT (T+2):
- Funds move from issuing bank → card network → acquiring bank → merchant account
- Standard settlement time in India: T+2 (2 business days)
- Razorpay, Stripe, PayPal all follow T+2 by default
- Instant settlement available at extra fee (0.25% to 1.5%)
- RBI is pushing for T+0 (same day) settlement for UPI

=======================================================================
INDIAN PAYMENT RAILS — THE INFRASTRUCTURE
=======================================================================

UPI (Unified Payments Interface):
- Built by NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India)
- Launched: April 2016
- Works 24x7x365, including holidays
- Transfers money in real time (within seconds)
- Limit: INR 1 lakh per transaction (some banks allow up to 2 lakh)
- No charge for P2P (person to person) transfers
- Merchant payments (P2M): 0% MDR below INR 2,000 (RBI mandate 2020)
- Above INR 2,000: MDR up to 0.3% for RuPay, 0.5-0.9% for Visa/Mastercard via UPI
- Daily transaction limit: INR 1 lakh (varies by bank)
- Monthly UPI transactions in India: 10+ billion (as of 2024)

IMPS (Immediate Payment Service):
- Real-time 24x7 transfers
- Limit: INR 5 lakh per transaction
- Charges: INR 5 to INR 25 per transaction (bank dependent)
- Used for: instant bank-to-bank transfers
- Identification: by mobile number + MMID, or account number + IFSC

NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer):
- Batch-based settlement (every 30 minutes, 24x7)
- No upper limit (as of 2020 RBI removed the limit)
- Charges: INR 0 to INR 25 depending on amount
- Below INR 10,000: INR 2.50
- INR 10,000 to 1 lakh: INR 5
- INR 1 lakh to 2 lakh: INR 15
- Above INR 2 lakh: INR 25
- Free for online banking (banks cannot charge for NEFT via internet/mobile)

RTGS (Real Time Gross Settlement):
- Minimum transfer: INR 2 lakh
- Settles transaction by transaction in real time (gross settlement)
- Used for large value transfers
- Available: 7 AM to 6 PM on working days
- Charges: INR 25 to INR 50 per transaction

SWIFT (Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication):
- Used for international wire transfers
- NOT a payment network — it's a messaging system between banks
- Banks send SWIFT messages instructing fund transfers
- Takes 2-5 business days for international transfers
- Fees: INR 500 to INR 2,500 per transfer + correspondent bank charges
- Used by: PayPal withdrawals, wire transfers from Outlier/foreign clients

CARD NETWORKS — WHO DOES WHAT
=======================================================================

VISA:
- Global card network
- Processes transactions between issuing and acquiring banks
- Takes a fee from each transaction (interchange fee)
- India: ~55% of credit card market
- Supports: credit, debit, prepaid cards

MASTERCARD:
- Second largest card network globally
- Similar to Visa in function
- India: ~35% of credit card market

RUPAY:
- India's domestic card network, built by NPCI
- Cheaper for merchants (lower MDR)
- Accepted mainly in India
- Cannot be used internationally (mostly)
- Promoted heavily by government for financial inclusion
- Zero MDR for transactions at government merchants

AMERICAN EXPRESS:
- Premium card network
- Higher fees than Visa/Mastercard
- Smaller acceptance network in India
- Higher rewards for cardholders offset by higher merchant fees

MDR (Merchant Discount Rate) — How it splits:
Total MDR = Issuing bank share + Card network share + Acquiring bank share + Payment gateway share
Example for a 2% MDR transaction of INR 1000:
- Issuing bank gets: ~1.1% (INR 11)
- Card network gets: ~0.1% (INR 1)
- Acquiring bank + gateway gets: ~0.8% (INR 8)
- Total: INR 20 deducted from merchant

=======================================================================
PAYMENT GATEWAYS VS PAYMENT PROCESSORS VS ACQUIRING BANKS
=======================================================================

PAYMENT GATEWAY (e.g., Razorpay, Stripe, PayPal):
- The technology layer that handles the transaction
- Encrypts card data
- Communicates with card networks
- Provides the checkout experience
- Charges MDR/transaction fee for this service

PAYMENT PROCESSOR:
- Executes the actual transfer of funds
- Often the same entity as the gateway in modern setups
- Handles clearing and settlement

ACQUIRING BANK:
- The bank of the merchant
- Holds the merchant account
- Receives funds on behalf of merchant
- Often works behind the payment gateway

ISSUING BANK:
- The bank that issued the customer's card/UPI account
- Approves or declines transactions
- Bears fraud risk (to some extent)

=======================================================================
PCI-DSS — PAYMENT SECURITY STANDARD
=======================================================================

PCI-DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard):
- Security standard for any entity that stores/processes/transmits card data
- Set by major card networks (Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Discover)
- 12 requirements covering network security, encryption, access control, monitoring
- Compliance required for ALL merchants and payment service providers
- Non-compliance can result in fines of $5,000-$100,000/month

Why it matters for ChargeClarity / Pine Labs:
- Pine Labs handles card transactions for thousands of merchants
- All data must be encrypted and stored securely
- This is why payment gateway APIs don't return full card numbers
- Tokenization: card numbers replaced with tokens to avoid storing sensitive data

CHARGEBACKS AND DISPUTES
=======================================================================

What is a chargeback?
- When a customer disputes a charge directly with their bank (not the merchant)
- The bank reverses the transaction and takes money back from the merchant
- Merchant also pays a chargeback fee (USD 15-25 depending on gateway)

Chargeback process:
1. Customer contacts bank, claims unauthorized/wrong charge
2. Bank reverses transaction immediately (provisional credit to customer)
3. Bank notifies payment gateway with a dispute
4. Merchant has 7-14 days to provide evidence (receipt, delivery proof, etc.)
5. If merchant wins: money returned, chargeback fee sometimes refunded
6. If merchant loses: transaction amount + fee permanently gone

Chargeback fees by platform:
- PayPal: USD 20 per dispute (non-refundable even if you win)
- Stripe: USD 15 per dispute (refunded if you win)
- Razorpay: INR 1,250 approximately

High chargeback rate consequences:
- If merchant chargeback rate exceeds 1% of transactions: warning
- If exceeds 2%: account termination risk
- Added to MATCH list (black list) — very hard to get another gateway

=======================================================================
FRAUD DETECTION IN PAYMENTS
=======================================================================

How payment gateways detect fraud:
- Velocity checks: same card used multiple times in short window
- Geolocation: IP address vs billing address mismatch
- Device fingerprinting: suspicious device signals
- Machine learning models: pattern matching against known fraud patterns
- CVV/OTP verification: extra authentication layer
- 3D Secure (3DS): OTP sent to customer's registered mobile

India specific: RBI mandates 2FA (Two Factor Authentication) for all online transactions
- Card number + CVV is not enough
- OTP to registered mobile is mandatory for most transactions
- This is why Indian users get OTP prompts for every online payment

Stripe Radar (free with all Stripe accounts):
- ML-based fraud detection
- Blocks suspicious transactions automatically
- Generates a risk score for each transaction
- Advanced Radar: USD 0.02/transaction additional for more controls

=======================================================================
WALLETS AND PREPAID INSTRUMENTS
=======================================================================

Paytm Wallet / MobiKwik / PhonePe Wallet:
- Prepaid Payment Instruments (PPI) regulated by RBI
- Load money from bank, spend from wallet
- P2P transfers: free up to INR 10,000/month, 1% above that
- Merchant payments: similar to UPI
- Full KYC wallet: up to INR 1 lakh balance
- Minimum KYC wallet: up to INR 10,000 balance

Loading wallet fees:
- From bank account/UPI: free
- From credit card: 1.5% to 2% fee typically

BNPL (BUY NOW PAY LATER) IN INDIA
=======================================================================

Major players: ZestMoney, LazyPay, Simpl, Amazon Pay Later, Flipkart Pay Later
How it works:
- Customer pays nothing at checkout
- BNPL provider pays merchant immediately (minus 2-3% fee)
- Customer repays BNPL provider in installments
- If customer defaults: BNPL provider bears the risk (not the merchant)
Merchant cost: 2-3% of transaction (similar to credit card)
Growing rapidly in India: USD 3.7 billion market as of 2024
